💬 Essential Travel Phrases

🗣️ Michigan Slang & Local Expressions

🎤 SPEAKING ACTIVITY: Read each slang term, then tell a story using it!
🥤 Pop (not soda!)

Meaning: Soft drinks like Coke or Pepsi

Use it: "Can I get a pop?" or "I'd love a cold pop!"

🎤 Practice: Order a drink using "pop" in a sentence

🌲 Up North

Meaning: Northern Michigan vacation areas with lakes and cabins

Use it: "We're heading up north for the weekend!"

🎤 Practice: Describe a vacation using "up north"

🧤 The Mitten

Meaning: Michigan's Lower Peninsula (shaped like a mitten)

Use it: "I'm visiting the Mitten state!" or "Show me on your hand!" (locals use their hand as a map)

🎤 Practice: Use your hand to show Michigan locations!

📍 Where at?

Meaning: "Where?" (Michiganders add "at")

Use it: "There's a concert tonight!" "Oh cool, where at?"

🎤 Practice: Ask questions using "where at?"

🏔️ Yooper

Meaning: Someone from Michigan's Upper Peninsula (U.P.)

Use it: "Are you a Yooper?"

🍫 Fudgie

Meaning: Tourist who visits lakeside towns and buys fudge

Use it: "I'm totally a fudgie - I need to try that famous Mackinac fudge!"

🎤 Practice: Tell a funny story about being a tourist

✅ Yeah no / No yeah

Meaning: "Yeah no" = No, "No yeah" = Yes (the last word counts!)

Use it: "Want to go?" "No yeah!" (means yes!)

🎤 Practice: Use these in quick responses to questions

👀 Lookit

Meaning: "Look at it" (said quickly)

Use it: "I found a Petoskey stone!" "Wow, lookit!"

🎭 Role-Play Scenarios

🎤 YOUR TIME TO SHINE: Choose a scenario and speak for 2-3 minutes!

Your teacher will play the other person. Focus on speaking naturally and asking questions!

Choose Your Scenario:

🏨 Hotel Check-in Scenario

Your role: You're checking into your hotel in Traverse City. You're tired from traveling.

What to do:
  • Greet the receptionist
  • Give your reservation name
  • Ask about hotel amenities (pool, breakfast, WiFi)
  • Ask for restaurant recommendations
  • Ask what time checkout is
  • Ask about parking
Useful phrases: "I have a reservation..." / "Could you tell me about..." / "What time does... ?" / "Is there a...?"

🍽️ Restaurant Ordering Scenario

Your role: You're at a popular Michigan restaurant. You want to try local food!

What to do:
  • Greet the server
  • Ask for recommendations (Michigan specialties)
  • Ask about ingredients you don't know
  • Order drinks using "pop" if appropriate
  • Order your meal
  • Ask about dessert
  • Request the check and ask about tipping
Michigan foods to try: Cherry pie, whitefish, pasties (meat pies), Coney dogs, Mackinac fudge

🗺️ Asking for Directions Scenario

Your role: You're lost in downtown Detroit. You need to find the Motown Museum.

What to do:
  • Politely get someone's attention
  • Ask for directions
  • Ask if it's walkable
  • Ask about how long it takes
  • Ask if there's a bus or shuttle
  • Thank them warmly
  • Ask one more question about what else to see nearby
Try using: "Excuse me, could you help me?" / "Where at?" / "Is it within walking distance?"

🛍️ Gift Shop Scenario

Your role: You're in a Mackinac Island fudge shop. You want souvenirs for family.

What to do:
  • Browse and ask to try samples
  • Ask about different flavors
  • Ask about prices
  • Joke about being a "fudgie"
  • Ask what's most popular
  • Ask about shipping options
  • Make your purchase and ask about local recommendations
Be friendly! Chat with the shopkeeper, ask questions, make jokes about being a tourist!

🚤 Booking a Tour Scenario

Your role: You want to book a kayak tour at Pictured Rocks.

What to do:
  • Ask about available tours
  • Ask about difficulty level (you're a beginner)
  • Ask what to bring
  • Ask about weather and if tours get cancelled
  • Ask about duration and price
  • Ask if you need to know how to swim
  • Book your tour and confirm the details
Show enthusiasm! "That sounds amazing!" / "I'm really excited to try this!" / "I've heard great things about..."

👋 Meeting a Local Scenario

Your role: You meet a friendly local at a beach. They're curious about your trip!

What to do:
  • Introduce yourself and say where you're from
  • Tell them why you're visiting Michigan
  • Ask for their recommendations
  • Ask about local slang or expressions
  • Share what you've enjoyed so far
  • Ask about their favorite Michigan places
  • Exchange contact info or social media
Be conversational! Ask questions back, show interest, use the slang you learned! Mention Brazil!

🌤️ June Weather Tips

Temperature: 65-80°F (18-27°C) - warm and comfortable!

What to bring:

  • Layers - mornings can be cool, afternoons warm
  • Swimsuit - lakes are swimmable by June!
  • Sunscreen - strong summer sun
  • Light rain jacket - occasional showers
  • Comfortable walking shoes
